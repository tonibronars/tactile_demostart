Leveraging Touch for Dexterous Behavior Discovery and Deployment

Antonia Bronars1, Nimrod Gileadi2, Jose Enrique Chen2, Zilin Si3, Francesco Nori2, Nicolas Heess2, Maria Bauza2
1MIT, 2Google DeepMind 3Carnegie Mellon University
Paper

Touch helps us discover robust behaviors in simulation using demonstration-guided RL that transfer better to the real world zero-shot.

Abstract

We study the influence of contact information in solving a high-precision plug insertion using a robot arm equipped with a dexterous hand. We discover contact-rich, dexterous behaviors to reorient, grasp, and insert the plug combining tactile and reinforcement learning (RL) in simulation. We then distill the RL policy with behavior cloning, and find that contact information is crucial for efficiently learning behaviors that transfer well to the real world. We also study and discuss the importance of exposing the behavior cloning agent to contact information. Ultimately, we demonstrate a 6x performance improvement over prior work through our improved behavior discovery and deployment pipeline.
